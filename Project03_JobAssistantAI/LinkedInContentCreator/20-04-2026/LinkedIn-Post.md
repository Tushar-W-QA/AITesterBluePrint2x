Most teams catch production bugs 3 weeks too late.
We caught ours before the developers even wrote the first line of code.
The difference was moving one meeting on the calendar.

It happens in almost every sprint.
QA gets the build on Thursday, tests frantically, and finds gaps in the logic.
The release is delayed, and everyone is frustrated.
I watched this cycle repeat until we stopped testing the code and started testing the requirements.

We moved QA into the requirement analysis phase.
Instead of finding edge cases in UAT, we found them in sprint refinement.
Developers received clearer acceptance criteria from day one.

But here is the part that stuck with me.
We didn't buy a new tool or change our tech stack.
We improved defect detection by 30% just by changing when we asked questions.

Shift-left testing isn't a tool you install.
It's a mindset you adopt.

The cheapest bug to fix is the one you never write.
