100% test coverage is the most dangerous metric in software testing.

I watched a team celebrate "perfect" coverage for three months straight. Then a single edge case in the payment flow wiped out $40k in revenue because they missed a critical business path entirely.

Coverage tells you how much code was touched.
It does not tell you if the right things were tested.

I’ve seen too many dashboards decorated with 95% green bars while the defect escape rate remains high. We are optimizing for the wrong variable. Coverage is a map, not the destination.

But here is the part that stuck with me: the team wasn't lazy. They were just following the metric they were told to hit. They were measuring testing, not quality.

What actually matters is risk-based prioritization. You test what breaks the business first, not just what's easiest to write a unit test for. Every test must map to a real user need, or it’s just noise in your pipeline.

Metrics should drive decisions, not decorate dashboards.

Is your team measuring quality — or just measuring testing?
